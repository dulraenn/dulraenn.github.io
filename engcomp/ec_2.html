Simple, Every-day Things You Can Do

Eat less beef & lamb

I'm not saying to go vegan overnight– just reduce. Cows and sheep produce a lot of methane. Swapping out red meat once or twice a week helps much more than you'd think.

Turn down your water heater by a few degrees

For example, turning your water heater from 140°F to 120°F. Doing this will save you energy every day, automatically, and you'll hardly realize.

Unplug unused electronics

Things like toasters, game consoles, monitors, chargers, etc. draw power even when they're "off." You can unplug them or, for a simpler solution, plug them all into a power strip and then just flip the switch off before you go to bed. You'll be saving both energy and a bit of money.

Go paperless for mail, bills, receipts

There's no reason to be receiving a tree's worth of paper every week– most banks, stores, and other companies will let you opt out. It only takes a couple minutes and saves a lot of waste in the long term.

Support local climate policy

Even just voting or emailing a representative when big bills are being voted on can make a difference. Systemic change beats 10,000 reuseable straws.

Walk or bike short distances

Even if it's just on occasion. Not only are car trips under 3 miles terrible for efficiency, the simple fact of there being less emissions is a plus.
