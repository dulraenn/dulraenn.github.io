Why It Matters

Not even small actions happen in isolation. When one person takes climate-friendly steps– like recycling more, driving less, or conserving energy– it’s not just about the action itself. It’s about what that action shows others.

Studies have found that people are far more likely to take action when they feel like their behavior influences a larger movement. This is the “ripple effect.” One person’s efforts can inspire others around them to make similar choices, setting off a chain reaction.

“If individuals can be taught about the ways in which their individual efforts influence their peers, businesses, and governments, then their perception of efficacy will increase.”
— Hornsey et al.

Individual action is contagious. And that matters much more than people think.
